GOVERNANCE KNOWLEDGE BASE

Governance refers to the processes, systems, and structures used to manage and direct organizations, institutions, or governments. It ensures accountability, transparency, and effective decision making.

Types of Governance:
1. Political Governance – Management of public policies and government functions.
2. Corporate Governance – Rules and practices guiding companies.
3. Digital Governance – Use of technology in government services.

Principles of Good Governance:
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Rule of law
- Participation
- Responsiveness

Government Responsibilities:
- Maintain law and order
- Provide public services
- Develop infrastructure
- Protect citizens' rights

E-Governance:
E-Governance uses digital technologies to improve government services such as online applications, digital identity systems, and electronic voting.

Effective governance improves public trust and supports sustainable development.The Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is a national mission for financial inclusion to ensure access to financial services, namely, basic savings & deposit accounts, remittance, credit, insurance, and pension in an affordable manner.

To apply for a national passport, citizens must submit proof of identity, proof of address, and proof of date of birth online through the official passport portal, followed by an in-person appointment at the Passport Seva Kendra.

The Right to Information (RTI) Act mandates timely response to citizen requests for government information. Citizens can file an RTI application through the official portal with a nominal fee to request records from any government authority.
