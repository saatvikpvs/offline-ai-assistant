MEDICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE

Medicine is the science and practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing diseases. Healthcare systems aim to maintain and improve the health of individuals and communities.

Common Types of Diseases:
1. Infectious Diseases – Caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites.
2. Chronic Diseases – Long-term illnesses such as diabetes and heart disease.
3. Genetic Disorders – Conditions inherited through genes.

Basic Health Practices:
- Maintain balanced nutrition
- Exercise regularly
- Get adequate sleep
- Maintain hygiene

Healthcare Services:
- Primary care: basic medical consultation
- Secondary care: specialized treatment
- Emergency care: urgent medical attention

Important Medical Technologies:
- Medical imaging (MRI, CT scans)
- Telemedicine
- Electronic health records
- AI-assisted diagnosis

Preventive healthcare focuses on avoiding disease through vaccination, healthy lifestyle, and early detection.Common cold symptoms usually peak within 2-3 days and include sneezing, stuffy nose, and sore throat. Rest and hydration are the best remedies. Antibiotics do not cure viral infections.

A fever is considered to be any body temperature above 100.4°F (38°C). It is usually a sign that your body is fighting off an infection. Drinking lots of fluids is highly recommended.

First aid for minor burns involves cooling the burn under running room-temperature water for 10-15 minutes. Never apply ice directly, as it can damage the tissue further. Do not pop blisters.
